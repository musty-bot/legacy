About Hands of Hope

Hands of Hope is a community based organisation in Kakamega, Kenya, working to foster empowerment to the disadvantages and marginalized youth through arts, sports, movies creation and dialogue.

We are champions for social justice and advocating for basic rights like shelter, food, education and health. Our activities like sports, arts, movies creation and dialogue are instrumental in advocating against negative cultural practices as well as systems of political injustice, child labour, sexual abuse and gender based violence (GBV).

We believe in creating opportunities for youth to express themselves, develop skills, and become agents of change in their communities.

Our Key Activities

Arts, Movies Creation and Dialogue

Training youth and encouraging them to positively use digital platforms to create awareness and give voice to issues affecting them.

Social Injustice Advocacy

Championing and advocating for socio-economic accountability and political justice for youth and families affected by political injustice.

Community Health and Awareness

Partnering with health organizations to create awareness on lifestyle diseases, HIV prevention, and organizing free medical screenings.

Child Right Protection

Training children aged 8-18 years on their rights, life skills and arts to encourage school enrollment and prevent street children.
